General Questions

Solar panels allow particles of light (photons) to knock electrons free from atoms, generating a flow of electricity. This Direct Current (DC) is then converted by an inverter to Alternating Current (AC), which is what runs the appliances in your home.

No, solar panels require sunlight to generate electricity. However, with a battery storage system or net metering, you can use stored energy or credits from excess daytime generation to power your home at night.

Installation & Maintenance

When installed correctly by professionals, solar panels actually protect the portion of the roof they cover. We use specialized mounting equipment that ensures structural integrity and waterproofing is maintained.

In dusty environments, we recommend cleaning them once every 2-3 months. Often, normal rainfall is enough to keep them performing well. You can simply hose them down if they get too dirty.

Costs & Finance

The typical ROI or payback period for a residential solar system is 3 to 5 years. Given that panels last 25+ years, this means you get free electricity for over 20 years.
